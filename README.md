# OpenCreator

> **You create. OpenCreator preserves and shares..**

OpenCreator is a free and open-source environment designed for independent creators.

Its purpose is simple:

Help creators focus on creating while OpenCreator handles organization, formats, publishing and long-term preservation.

OpenCreator does not replace existing open-source software.

It connects proven tools into one simple, human-centered environment.

---

# Why OpenCreator?

Creating a book, a website, a podcast, a video or an online event often requires dozens of different applications, formats and platforms.

Most creators don't want to become system administrators.

They simply want to create.

OpenCreator lets creators express their intentions.

OpenCreator selects the appropriate tools, prepares the required formats, preserves the project locally and assists publication.

---

# Philosophy

OpenCreator is built around a few simple principles.

- Technology should serve creators.
- Local comes first.
- Open protocols come first.
- Free/Open Source Software comes first.
- Decentralized networks are preferred whenever possible.
- Transparency is essential.
- Simplicity is a design requirement.
- Projects belong to their creators.
- OpenCreator remains modular and adaptable.
- Software changes. Human needs remain.

# Our ambition

We believe technology should become simpler.

We believe creators should remain independent.

We believe local ownership and decentralized sharing can coexist.

OpenCreator aims to become a sustainable environment that helps creators produce, preserve and transmit their work for many years.

---

# Open by design

OpenCreator relies on:

- Open Standards
- Free/Open Source Software
- Human-readable formats
- Local-first storage
- Modular architecture
- Community contributions

Whenever possible, OpenCreator favors decentralized technologies over centralized services.
