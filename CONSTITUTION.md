# OpenCreator Constitution

> Technology should serve creators.

This Constitution defines the long-term principles of OpenCreator.

Software evolves.
Hardware changes.
Online platforms come and go.

These principles are intended to remain stable.

---

# Principle 1 — The creator comes first

The creator expresses intentions.

OpenCreator handles the technical complexity whenever possible.

Technology should adapt to people.

Never the opposite.

---

# Principle 2 — Local-first

Every project begins and lives locally.

The creator always keeps control of their work.

Online services extend local creation.
They never replace it.

---

# Principle 3 — Open first

Whenever possible, OpenCreator gives priority to:

- Free and Open Source Software
- Open standards
- Open formats
- Decentralized protocols
- Community-driven projects

Commercial ecosystems remain optional gateways, never the foundation of OpenCreator.

---

# Principle 4 — Preserve before sharing

Creation is valuable.

Long-term preservation always comes before sharing.

OpenCreator gives priority to open and decentralized networks.

Commercial platforms remain available whenever creators choose to use them.

---

# Principle 5 — One project, many formats

Creators should not have to recreate the same work multiple times.

OpenCreator prepares and converts formats whenever possible.

Create once.

Preserve forever.

Share everywhere.

---

# Principle 6 — Human language

Creators describe what they want to achieve.

Not which software they want to use.

OpenCreator selects the appropriate tools whenever possible.

---

# Principle 7 — Simplicity

Every feature must reduce complexity.

Never increase it.

The technology should become almost invisible.

---

# Principle 8 — Modular architecture

Software evolves.

OpenCreator remains modular.

Components may be replaced by better free alternatives without redesigning the whole environment.

OpenCreator does not replace existing free software.

It connects, simplifies and extends it.

---

# Principle 9 — Durable knowledge

Projects should remain understandable for many years.

Human-readable formats are preferred.

Documentation is considered part of every project.

Knowledge deserves the same care as code.

---

# Principle 10 — Community

OpenCreator grows through cooperation.

Knowledge is shared.

Experience is transmitted.

Everyone can contribute.

Small contributions build strong communities.

---

# Principle 11 — Support creators

OpenCreator helps creators receive support in many forms:

- knowledge
- collaborations
- shared creations
- equipment
- financial contributions
- local exchanges
- cryptocurrencies
- traditional payments

Technology facilitates human relationships.

It never replaces them.

---

# Principle 12 — Freedom of choice

OpenCreator recommends solutions.

The creator always makes the final decision.

Freedom includes the freedom to choose another tool, another workflow or another destination.

---

# Principle 13 — Create. Preserve. Transmit.

Every decision made within OpenCreator should answer one simple question:

Does it help creators create,preserve or transmit their work?

If not,

it probably does not belong here.

---

# Principle 14 — Integrate, don't reinvent

OpenCreator does not aim to replace mature Free/Open Source software.

Whenever a stable, well-maintained solution already exists, OpenCreator prefers to integrate it rather than recreate it.

Our goal is to simplify the creator's experience by connecting proven tools into one coherent environment.

Software may evolve.

Principles remain.

OpenCreator values interoperability, open standards and long-term sustainability over unnecessary duplication.simplification and interoperability over creating another competing application.
